Guidelines

Below is an example showing how to annotate an English sentence.

First you should register an account, you should always annotate after logging in.

On home page, click on login.

This is the login page, type in your Email and password.

After logging in, click annotate on Nav bar:

Choose a txt file, flex format xml or tool box format xml, choose a language, then click on both Submit and Annotate! buttons

At the following annotation page, you can select "tasted" in the sentence by double clicking or click and drag. Then there is a button appears after Concept: click the "tasted" button.

In the submenu, hover over find lemma, and click on tasted-01 in the subsubmenu:

Then the first line of annotation (t / taste-01) appears under UMR Penman Annotation:

Technically the first concept you annotate will be assumed to be the head, but you can set head by select the variable name, and click on set head submenu:

Add Aspect attribute as following, it's very important you click on submit button every time you choose an option:

After submit :Aspect option, the attribute value box will appear, you can choose Performance in it, again, you should click on submit after selecting:

The second line of annotation appears under UMR Penman Annotation:

Next, let's add :ARG0

Add a Person Named Entity Type:

Select Edmund Pope, hover over find lemma on submenu, click on Edmund Pope on subsubmenu

If you want to logout or go annotate another sentence or file, and then come back, click on db button before you go

You can switch to another sentence by selecting options in Sentence ID box:

When you come back to sentence 1, the UMR Penman Annotation is empty:

You can click on load history button, the history annotated will appear:

After you finish your annotation, click on save button, there is form appears at the bottom of the page

Type in the file name and click on save locally, you should see your downloaded file in your download folder:

If you see a 500 page, make sure you logged in and try refresh after a few seconds, sometimes it helps, sometimes it doesn't ¯\_(ツ)_/¯ I am working on fixing it
